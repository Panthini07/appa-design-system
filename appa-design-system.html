1 · Colors

Signature gradient
The luminous bridge between teal and pink. Used for the app logo, hero moments, loading indicators, and celebratory states · never as a background.
Primary · Teal · 4 tokens
Primary color of action. Teal carries the most UI surface area · buttons, nav, brand, selected states. Hierarchy: primary → primary-bright → primary-strong → primary-tint. Reserve full-saturation --primary (#11D2CB) for actions and CTAs; use --primary-tint or lighter shades for ambient, high-frequency, and background elements so the UI stays calm.

7 · Brand & Motion

Logo & app icon
Appa's mark: a teal check rising out of a white tile dotted with soft clouds, wordmark below. Keep clear space of at least half the tile height on every side. Minimum 24px in-app; 44px app-icon floor. Place on white or a solid surface chip, never on busy photography. Don't recolour the teal, restretch, or add shadows.
Primary mark
Monochrome
App icon · 44px min
Do · keep clear space · use on a solid chip · scale proportionally
Don't · stretch, recolour the teal, add shadows, or place on busy photos
Imagery
Photography
Authentic, warm, everyday moments. No stock perfection. Slightly cool (not warm) grading to match the luminous blue-white surface. Ratios: 16:9 feed · 1:1 avatar · 4:5 full-screen.
Illustration
Flat, rounded, character-forward. No complex gradients. Functional illustrations (empty states, onboarding) stay on-brand · teal + pink, single accent each. Celebration / reward illustrations open up to a wider palette: bright, solid colours · no dull colours · so wins feel special.
Motion
Motion feels like a calm exhale on entry, a small jolt of joy on exit. Spring easing for playful moments (button press, task check-off). Ease-out for all enters. Hover-revealed popovers use a short intent delay (~200ms, --delay-hover) before they appear and a fast exit, so an accidental swipe doesn't pop them up. Always respect prefers-reduced-motion.

Tone & personality
✓ Do
  • Be present, friendly, minimal
  • Be playful, sometimes funny
  • Open calm, exit actionable
  • Mirror the user's own words
  • Celebrate joyful micro-moments (task check-off)
  • Get out of the way fast
✗ Don't
  • Overwhelm or clutter
  • Be authoritative or preachy
  • Trigger avoidance (via guilt, shame, shocking language)
  • Use generic productivity clichés
  • Write dense walls of text
  • Use aggressive or urgency nudges

9 · Content & UX Writing

Voice in practice
Appa's edge is language. The rule: name the feeling, shrink the task, hand back control. Short, warm, never guilt / shame / urgency. Mirror the user's own words.
ContextInstead ofWrite
ErrorSomething went wrong. Try again.That didn't go through · no stress, give it another tap.
Empty stateNo tasks found.Nothing here yet. Your first small step starts a space.
OverdueYou missed 3 deadlines!3 things are waiting whenever you're ready.
NudgeDon't break your progress!Want to do the 2-minute version today?
CelebrationTask completed.Done. That was easy · nice one. 🎉
Primary buttonSubmitPick me up · Start small · I'll try
DismissCancelMaybe later
✓ Words we use
small step · gently · whenever you're ready · no pressure · try · one thing · breathe · nice one
✗ Words we avoid
must · should · failed · don't · deadline · hurry · lazy · finally · behind
Emoji usage
Emoji add warmth · but sparingly. At most one per message or label, never mid-sentence as decoration. Use them for emotion and celebration, not for status (status uses semantic colour + words).
✓ Do
🎉 celebrations · 🌱 growth / empty states · 💡 a tip · one trailing emoji on a warm line
✗ Don't
😰 fear / urgency · 🔥 to mean "you're behind" · stacking 3+ · emoji as the only label · replacing semantic colours
